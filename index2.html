Corners & chicanes

BeforeLimitName (optional)Chicane
Before = the space index the corner line sits in front of. Limit = the speed you must be at or under when you cross it.
Chicane — put two (or more) neighbouring corners in the same chicane and they become one chicane: blue kerbs, and the game draws a single Road Condition token for the pair. A corner token then modifies both corner lines; a sector token modifies the sector after the chicane. Each line is still checked separately at Check Corner. A chicane's corners must be consecutive and share the same speed limit.

Track order

Drag a row (or use ▲▼) to set the order the circuits appear in the game's track picker — or just long-press a track tab up top and drag it. The order is saved with your edits and written straight into the downloaded game file.

Gravel & Tunnels

Pick a brush, then tap or sweep spaces on the board to paint them. Tap a painted space again to undo it. In = the Spot nearest the Race Line, Out = the outside Spot — gravel can sit on either or both. Tunnels cover whole Spaces and are stored as runs, so ranges wrap past the start line (70-4 is fine). Typing in the boxes works too: 12-15, 22.

Scenery & AI art prompt

Pick a feature, then Draw zone (drag a box), Freehand (sketch any outline with your finger — it closes itself when you lift), or Add at centre. Tap a zone to select it — while selected, one finger moves it and the board won't scroll (two fingers still pinch/pan). Drag any of the 8 handles to reshape/resize; corner handles resize both ways, edge handles stretch one side. ◯/▢/✎ flips organic patch / hard-edged block (freehand shapes flatten to a patch). ▲/▼ in the list changes layering — ▲ brings a zone on top of the ones before it (e.g. put the boat above the beach). Delete removes it. Generate writes a full image prompt: attach a clean screenshot of this board (photo + ribbon on, numbers on) to ChatGPT alongside it, and it will paint the wasteland around your track without touching the racing line.

Pace simulator

A greedy solo bot plays the real Heat rules — shifts gears, boosts on the straights, brakes for corners, pays Heat when it runs a corner hot and spins out when it can't. Running it a few hundred times shows expected rounds per lap, where spin-outs cluster, and any corner that's never actually threatened (a limit doing no work). Turn on the overlay to paint each corner badge by the Heat it costs.
